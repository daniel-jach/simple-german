European Union (EU) The European Union (EU for short) is an union of European states that pursue common political goals. The EU has existed since 1 November, 1993. On this day, 12 countries, including Germany, joined together to form the European Union, as had been previously agreed in the Maastricht Treaty. Before this, European states had already banded together to form the so-called European Community. However, it had other goals than those of the EU, and was differently organised. Every country in Europe has the chance to join the European Union. However, it must fulfil several conditions first. One of the most important of these is that the country that wants to join must be a democracy. The countries that have joined to form the EU want to work together on many political, economic and social issues. They want to ensure that Europes wealth is maintained and that its people can live in peace. Sometimes, coming up with common policies is rather complicated. All the nations in the EU are still independent states and have their own governments. So there are occasionally quarrels between the EU and the individual nations  and, of course, between the nations themselves. Its like in a big family: reaching an agreement is not always easy.
